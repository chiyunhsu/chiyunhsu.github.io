Baby Algebraic Geometry Seminar

Mondays 5-6 in SC 530

  • February 112013 Science Center 530Harvard Gabriel Bujokas
    The degree of the Severi variety This will be a gentle introduction to the Severi variety and the technique introduced by Caporaso and Harris to compute its degree. We will motivate their approach by looking at the case of singular cubics, and then use it to work out a more interesting example.
  • February 182013 Science Center 530Harvard Francesco Cavazzani
    Kapranov's theorem on rational normal curves It is classically known that there is only one twisted cubic passing through 6 general points in the projective space. What if we have only 5 points? Kapranov gives a beautiful description of the variety parametrizing such curves, in terms of the moduli space of abstract pointed curves. We will use this result to answer an easy enumerative problem involving a secancy condition.
  • February 252013 Science Center 530Harvard Tiankai Liu
    Noether's Theorem on the Cremona group of the Plane Max Noether is generally credited with the theorem that the group of birational automorphisms of the complex projective plane is generated by projective linear transformations and Cremona involutions. Typical of nineteenth-century algebraic geometry, his proof was subjected to a slew of objections and emendations. We will discuss this theorem from the modern viewpoint of the Sarkisov program.
  • March 42013 Science Center 530Harvard Atanas Atanasov
    The ADE classification and beyond The aim of this talk will be to introduce rational double point singularities on surfaces and discuss various aspects of their classification. As time permits, we will motivate several related local-to-global questions about quotient singularities and talk about potential approaches.

  • April 12013 Science Center 530Harvard Matthew Woolf
    Birational geometry of the space of one-dimensional sheaves on the plane Given a line bundle on a smooth plane curve, we can push it forward and think of it as a sheaf in the plane. In this talk we will see how this idea allows us to construct a natural compactification of the relative Jacobian of the universal plane curve. We will then study some aspects of the divisor theory of these spaces.
  • April 82013 Science Center 530Harvard Nathan Pflueger
    Weierstrass points A smooth algebraic curve of genus g has a finite number of special points, called Weierstrass points, which are those points P such that the divisor g \cdot P moves in a pencil. Each Weierstrass point determines a numerical semigroup. I will discuss some recent results on the inverse problem: given a numerical semigroup, how large is the space of pointed curves corresponding to Weierstrass points with that semigroup? Time permitting, I will discuss some related problems in Brill-Noether theory.
  • April 152013 Science Center 530Harvard Eric Riedl
    Subvarieties of hypersurfaces of moderately high degree In this talk we'll discuss which varieties can be subvarieties of hypersurfaces of moderately high degree. For instance, a general hypersurface of degree at least 2n-2 contains no rational curves. We discuss some results of Ein and Voisin generalizing this statement about rational curves.
