Algebraic Geometry Learning Seminar On Algebraic Stacks

Spring 2014
Wednesdays 3-4 SC 309

  • February 52014 Science Center 530Harvard Alex Perry
    Introduction to Algebraic Stacks

    I'll briefly motivate the study of algebraic stacks, and then start developing the categorical lannguage needed to define them. In this first lecture I plan to show the characterization of schemes as functors from AffSch to Sets. The material is quite dry, but necessary to do things precisely, and we'll try to get through it quickly.

    Notes from Alex Perry, from Melody Chan and from Ken Ascher.
  • February 122014 Science Center 530Harvard Tiankai Liu
    Sites and fibered categories

    We dryly present some technical prerequisites for future talks in this series. Our talk is divided in two seemingly unrelated parts. In the first part, we define Grothendieck topologies on categories, and sheaves on sites. In the second part, we define the 2-category of of fibered categories over an arbitrary base category C, and state the 2-Yoneda's lemma. Time permitting, we will make some remarks on categories fibered in sets or, more generally, groupoids.

    Notes from Tiankai Liu and from Ken Ascher.
  • February 192014 Science Center 530Harvard Adrian Zahariuc
    Faithfully flat descent

    We will discuss some definitions and technical results about descent. Descent is the analogue of usual glueing properties. We will state and sketch the proof of the theorem that representable functors are sheaves in Sch_Y with the fppf topology. Next, we will talk about effective descent morphisms and faithfully flat descent for quasi-coherent sheaves, if time permits.

    Notes from Melody Chan and from Ken Ascher.
  • February 262013 Science Center 530Harvard Gabriel Bujokas
    Examples of algebraic spaces

    We will define algebraic spaces, and walk through some cool examples (and perhaps see a few theorems on the way).

    Notes from Ken Ascher.

    More algebraic spaces

    Today, we'll talk more about algebraic spaces, and their relations to schemes. We will discuss what sort of facts about schemes still work for algebraic spaces, and what don't. Time permitting, we will also discuss some of the places where algebraic spaces occur "in nature".

    Notes from Matthew Woolf> and from Ken Ascher.

  • March 192014 NO SEMINAR SPRING BREAK
  • March 262014 Science Center 309Harvard Barbara Bolognese
    Algebraic stacks - a few examples in algebraic geometry

    After a brief recollection of what an algebraic stack is, we will give a few examples of algebraic stacks of great interest in algebraic geometry. More precisely, we will define quotient stacks (relatively to the action of some algebraic group on a scheme), we will define the stack of n-pointed stable curves, show that it is a Deligne-Mumford stack isomorphic to the quotient stack associated to the action of an algebraic group on some Hilbert scheme. Finally, we will define the moduli stack of primitively polarized K3 surfaces and show that it is a Deligne-Mumford stack.

    Notes from Nasko Atanasov.
  • April 22014 Science Center 309Harvard Francesco Cavazzani
    Quasi-Coherent Sheaves on Stacks

    After some refreshing of some definitions, we want to describe sheaves (and in particular, quasi coherent sheaves) on algebraic stacks. We will work on the Lisse-Etale topology (and topos), that basically allows us to define a sheaf on a stack X only using sheaves on schemes that have a smooth map to X. If time permits, as main example, we would like to describe Mumford's calculation of the Picard group of the moduli stack M_1,1 of elliptic pointed curves.

    Notes from Ken Ascher.
  • April 92014 Science Center 309Harvard Ken Ascher
    Relative Spec, Relative Proj & Root Stacks

    We will introduce 3 constructions related to stacks: relative spec, relative proj, and root stacks - the first two serving to generalize familiar notions from schemes. We will then define root stacks motivated by the following problem: given an effective Cartier divisor D on a scheme X and an integer n, does there exist an effective Cartier divisor E on X such that nE=D?

    Notes from Ken Ascher.
