  • October 32013 SC 507Harvard Gabriel Bujokas
    Hurwitz spaces We will study the Hurwitz space parametrizing simply ramified covers of a fixed curve X. We will review the classical results where X is rational, and ask the analogous questions for higher genus curves X.
  • October 102013 SC 507Harvard Chao Li
    Rational points on elliptic curves and geometry of Shimura curves We begin by describing an explicit 2-descent result on a certain class of elliptic curves E over Q, which together with the famous conjecture of Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer, predicts that E should have algebraic rank one. Motivated by a conjectural construction of a rational point of infinite order on E, we are led to study Heegner points on certain Shimura curves. We will try to explain how to construct various explicit models of such a Shimura curve, exhibit their beautiful geometry and deduce arithmetic consequences as well.
  • October 172013 SC 507Harvard Eric Riedl
    Rational Curves on Hypersurfaces This talk will be about the results of Harris, Roth, and Starr on the dimension of rational curves in hypersurfaces of large dimension. I will start by describing the question, talking about what the expected answer is, why the question is hard, and give a couple of simple examples. Then, I will state their result, and sketch the technique that they use to prove it.
  • October 242013 SC 507Harvard Atanas Atanasov
    Smoothing algebraic space curves The aim of this talk will be to explore the following question: Which singular curves in P^3 occur as limits of smooth ones? While a complete classification is too much to ask for, we will provide some answers for particular nodal curves. The topic provides a practical environment to learn about deformation theory and elementary transformations of vector bundles.
  • October 312013 SC 507Harvard Yinbang Lin
    The moduli spaces of stable pairs Let X be a projective variety, E_0 a fixed coherent sheaf on X. We would to parametrize morphisms E_0 --> E of coherent sheaves. Before doing this, we need to introduce semistability conditions. By relating these to GIT semistability conditions, we are able to construct the moduli space parametrizing these semistable objects, via GIT. In the talk, you will see the general idea to construct a moduli space via the geometric invariant theory. This is work in progress.
  • November 72013 SC 530Harvard John Lesieutre
    The Cone Conjecture If X is a Fano variety, then the cone of curves NE(X) is a rational polyhedral cone. In the "next" case, X Calabi-Yau, this need not be the case, but the Kawamata-Morrison cone conjecture predicts that even if the cone isn't rational polyhedral, it's given by translates of some rational polyhedral subcone under automorphisms of X. I'll give some examples and sketch the proof in dimension 2, mostly following a couple papers by Totaro.
